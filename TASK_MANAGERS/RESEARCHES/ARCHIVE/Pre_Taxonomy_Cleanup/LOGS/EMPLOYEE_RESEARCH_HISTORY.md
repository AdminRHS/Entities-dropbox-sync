## Quality Scoring System

**Research quality assessed on 0-10 scale:**

| Score | Quality Level | Description |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Exceptional | Exceeds expectations, provides additional insights |
| 7-8 | High Quality | Meets all requirements, well-documented |
| 5-6 | Acceptable | Meets minimum requirements, some gaps |
| 3-4 | Needs Improvement | Missing key elements, requires revision |
| 0-2 | Unacceptable | Does not meet basic requirements |

**Quality factors evaluated:**
- Completeness of research scope
- Accuracy of information
- Quality of documentation
- Actionability of findings
- Integration with LIBRARIES/TAXONOMY
- Timeliness of completion

## Usage Guidelines

### When Assigning New Research
1. Check employee's research history in their section
2. Review average completion time for similar research types
3. Check quality scores for past similar assignments
4. Consider skill development opportunities
5. Avoid overloading high performers

### After Research Completion
1. Add entry to employee's completed tasks table
2. Assign quality score (0-10)
3. Update performance matrix if applicable
4. Record any tools/workflows added to LIBRARIES
5. Update average completion times
6. Record any new skills developed

### Monthly Reviews
1. Calculate monthly performance summary
2. Update top performers lists
3. Identify training needs based on quality scores
4. Recognize exceptional performers
5. Plan skill development assignments
