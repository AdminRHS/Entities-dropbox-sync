## Task 2: Conduct Thursday Interview

### Steps:
1. Review candidate's resume and application materials
2. Prepare interview questions based on role requirements
3. Set up interview space (virtual or physical) and test technology
4. Review company information and role details to answer candidate questions
5. Conduct the interview following structured format
6. Take detailed notes during the interview
7. Complete candidate evaluation form immediately after interview
8. Schedule follow-up discussion with team if needed
9. Send thank you message to candidate
10. Document next steps and timeline

### Resources and Links:
- Candidate resume and application
- Interview questions template
- Company information packet
- Candidate evaluation form
- Calendar/scheduling system

### Instructions:

1. **Pre-interview preparation (Day before)**:
   - Read through candidate's resume and application thoroughly
   - Highlight key points to discuss
   - Prepare specific questions related to their experience
   - Review the role requirements and company culture points
   
2. **Technical setup**:
   - If virtual: Test video conferencing software, microphone, camera
   - If in-person: Confirm meeting room reservation and prepare space
   - Have backup contact method ready (phone number)
   
3. **Interview structure**:
   - Introduction (5 min): Welcome, company overview, role overview
   - Candidate questions (10 min): Allow candidate to ask questions
   - Technical/behavioral questions (30-40 min): Structured Q&A
   - Company culture fit discussion (10 min)
   - Next steps explanation (5 min)
   
4. **During interview**:
   - Take notes on key responses
   - Observe communication skills, enthusiasm, cultural fit
   - Answer candidate questions honestly and thoroughly
   
5. **Post-interview**:
   - Complete evaluation form while impressions are fresh
   - Rate candidate on: technical skills, communication, cultural fit, enthusiasm
   - Document any concerns or highlights
   - Send thank you email within 24 hours
   - Schedule internal discussion if needed

## Task 3: Review and Validate Onboarding Test Questions

### Steps:
1. Review all 30 test questions for clarity and relevance
2. Check that questions cover all intended topics
3. Verify answer keys are accurate and complete
4. Test the questions with a colleague or review independently
5. Ensure questions are appropriate difficulty level for new hires
6. Format questions consistently (multiple choice, short answer, etc.)
7. Add instructions for test takers
8. Create grading rubric or answer key document
9. Set up test delivery method (online form, document, etc.)
10. Document expected completion time

### Resources and Links:
- Onboarding test document (30 questions)
- Answer key document
- Topic coverage checklist
- Test delivery platform (if using online tool)

### Instructions:

1. **Question review process**:
   - Read each question aloud to check for clarity
   - Ensure questions are unambiguous and have one clear answer
   - Verify that questions test actual knowledge, not trick questions
   - Check that questions align with onboarding materials covered
   
2. **Topic coverage verification**:
   - Create a checklist of topics that should be covered:
     - Company culture and values
     - Role-specific knowledge
     - Tools and systems
     - Processes and procedures
     - Team structure
     - Communication protocols
   - Verify each topic has appropriate question coverage
   
3. **Answer key validation**:
   - Review each answer for accuracy
   - Ensure answers match the correct questions
   - Add explanations for correct answers where helpful
   - Verify no typos or errors in answer key
   
4. **Formatting and presentation**:
   - Ensure consistent formatting throughout
   - Add clear instructions at the beginning
   - Include time estimate for completion
   - Add space for name, date, role
   - Number questions clearly
   
5. **Testing phase**:
   - Have a colleague or yourself take the test
   - Note any confusing questions
   - Time how long it takes to complete
   - Adjust difficulty if needed
   
6. **Final preparation**:
   - Create final version with all corrections
   - Set up delivery method (Dropbox link, online form, etc.)
   - Prepare grading instructions for evaluators
   - Document expected outcomes and passing criteria
