### HR-Specific Skills

**SKL-HR-001: Candidate Sourcing**
- **Description:** Finding qualified candidates through various channels
- **Channels:** LinkedIn, job boards, referrals, social media, portfolio sites

**SKL-HR-002: Candidate Screening**

- **Methods:** Resume review, portfolio review, initial phone screen
- **Criteria:** Skills match, experience level, culture fit, availability
- **Output:** Shortlist of qualified candidates

**SKL-HR-003: Interview Conducting**
- **Description:** Structured interviews to assess candidates
- **Types:** Phone screen, technical interview, behavioral interview, culture fit
- **Techniques:** STAR method, competency-based questions
- **Skills Assessed:** Technical skills, soft skills, motivation, fit

**SKL-HR-004: Candidate Assessment**
- **Description:** Evaluating candidate suitability for role
- **Methods:** Skills tests, portfolio review, reference checks
- **For Technical Roles:** Coding tests, system design, technical Q&A
- **For Creative Roles:** Portfolio presentation, design challenge

**SKL-HR-005: Offer Negotiation**
- **Description:** Negotiating compensation and terms with candidates
- **Components:** Salary, benefits, start date, remote/hybrid terms
- **Skills:** Negotiation, market knowledge, persuasion
- **Goal:** Win-win agreement, candidate acceptance

**SKL-HR-006: Onboarding Coordination**

## 5. Processes Library (Recruitment Workflows)

**Total Processes:** 428 (full library)
**Included Here:** HR recruitment workflows

### PROC-RECRUIT-001: Full Recruitment Cycle

**Process Name:** End-to-End Recruitment
**Duration:** 2-4 weeks typical
**Owner:** Recruiter (Viktoriia or Evgeniya)

**Steps:**
1. **Job Analysis** - Define role requirements, skills, experience
2. **Job Posting** - Create and post job description on relevant channels
3. **Candidate Sourcing** - Active search for qualified candidates
4. **Application Review** - Screen incoming applications
5. **Phone Screening** - Initial conversation with top candidates
6. **Interview Scheduling** - Coordinate interview with hiring manager
7. **Interviews** - Conduct structured interviews (1-3 rounds)
8. **Assessment** - Skills tests, portfolio review, references
9. **Decision** - Hiring manager decision with recruiter input
10. **Offer Preparation** - Draft offer letter, determine compensation
11. **Offer Extension** - Present offer to candidate
12. **Negotiation** - Discuss and finalize terms
13. **Offer Acceptance** - Candidate accepts, paperwork signed
14. **Onboarding** - Welcome new hire, setup, orientation

**Success Metrics:**
- Time to fill: < 4 weeks
- Candidate quality: Hiring manager satisfaction
- Offer acceptance rate: > 80%
- New hire retention: > 90% after 3 months

### PROC-RECRUIT-002: Creative Role Recruitment (Designers, Video Editors)

**Specialized Process for Creative Positions**

**Key Differences from Standard Recruitment:**
1. **Portfolio Review** (critical step) - Evaluate design quality, style, versatility
2. **Design Challenge** (optional) - Small paid project to assess skills
3. **Culture Fit for Creative Teams** - Collaboration style, feedback receptiveness
4. **Platform Presence** - Dribbble, Behance, ArtStation activity

**Portfolio Evaluation Criteria:**
- Quality of work (9/10 standard expected)
- Variety of projects (versatility)
- Recent work (active designer)
- Alignment with Remote Helpers style
- Case studies (thought process, not just final designs)

### PROC-RECRUIT-003: Technical Role Recruitment (Developers, AI Engineers)

**Specialized Process for Technical Positions**

**Key Differences from Standard Recruitment:**
1. **GitHub Profile Review** - Code quality, activity, contributions
2. **Technical Screen** - Live coding or take-home assignment
3. **System Design Interview** - For senior roles
4. **Technical Skills Assessment** - Specific frameworks, languages
5. **Culture Fit for Tech Teams** - Collaboration, code review style

**Technical Evaluation Criteria:**
- Code quality (clean, maintainable)
- Problem-solving approach
- Technology stack match
- Learning ability (adapts to new tools)
- Communication (explains technical concepts)

### PROC-RECRUIT-004: High-Volume Recruitment (Lead Generators)

**Process for Scaling LG Department (11 employees)**

**Optimizations for Volume:**
1. **Streamlined Screening** - Quick yes/no criteria
2. **Group Interviews** - Multiple candidates at once for initial
3. **Fast Decision-Making** - Same-day or next-day offers
4. **Rolling Onboarding** - Weekly cohorts

**LG-Specific Criteria:**
- Research skills (finding contacts/leads)
- Attention to detail (data accuracy)
- Persistence (outreach consistency)
- Tech-savvy (Apollo, LinkedIn, Apify tools)

## Recognition Patterns for HR Meetings

### Common Discussion Topics

**When processing HR meeting transcripts, look for:**

1. **Role Requirements**
   - "We need a UI/UX designer with Figma experience"
   - "Looking for senior backend developer"
   - Recognition: PROF-025 (UI/UX), PROF-005 (Backend Dev)

2. **Candidate Names**
   - "Let's interview Candidate A next week"
   - "Portfolio from Candidate B looks promising"
   - Recognition: Create candidate tracking, assign actions

3. **Recruitment Stages**
   - "Phone screen scheduled for Friday"
   - "Waiting on reference checks"
   - Recognition: PROC-RECRUIT-001 (steps 5, 8)

4. **Decision-Making**
   - "Let's extend an offer to Candidate C"
   - "Not a good fit for this role"
   - Recognition: ACT-OFFER-002, document decision

5. **Sourcing Channels**
   - "Found great candidates on Dribbble"
   - "LinkedIn search yielded 20 prospects"
   - Recognition: ACT-SOURCE-004 (Dribbble), ACT-SOURCE-001 (LinkedIn)

### Sample Meeting Analysis

**Transcript Snippet:**
> "Viktoriia: I screened 15 UI/UX designer applications this week. Found 3 strong candidates. One has excellent Figma skills and 5 years experience. Portfolio on Dribbble is impressive - 8.5/10 quality.
>
> Evgeniya: Great! Let's schedule phone screens with all three. For the Figma expert, can we also prepare a small design challenge? Maybe a landing page redesign?
>
> Viktoriia: Yes, I'll schedule calls for next Tuesday and Wednesday. Design challenge deadline 5 days from assignment."

**Recognized Entities:**
- **Professions:** PROF-025 (UI/UX Designer), PROF-019 (Recruiter - both employees)
- **Actions:** ACT-SCREEN-001 (Resume review - completed 15), ACT-INTERVIEW-001 (Schedule interviews), ACT-ASSESS-001 (Design challenge)
- **Processes:** PROC-RECRUIT-002 (Creative role recruitment - in progress)
- **Parameters:** PARAM-REC-004 (Design challenge: 5 days), PARAM-REC-008 (Portfolio evaluation: 8.5/10)
- **Skills:** SKL-HR-002 (Candidate screening), SKL-HR-004 (Candidate assessment)

**Action Items:**
1. **Viktoriia** - Schedule phone screens with 3 candidates (Tuesday/Wednesday next week)
2. **Viktoriia** - Prepare landing page redesign challenge for top candidate
3. **Both** - Conduct phone screens when scheduled
